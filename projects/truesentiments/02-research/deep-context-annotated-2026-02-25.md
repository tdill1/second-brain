## 7. Strategic Analysis

### What TS Does Well
1. **Authentic founder story** — Bev's narrative is genuine and relatable
2. **Clear niche** — gratitude + mindfulness + self-care, not trying to be everything
3. **Trademarked products** — MyGratiBox™ and Inspiration Apparel™ show brand investment
4. **Multiple revenue streams** — physical products, apparel, wellness programs
5. **Blog content quality** — when published, the writing is personal and engaging

### Growth Opportunities
1. **Revive the blog** — consistent publishing (2x/month minimum) with SEO-optimized posts could drive significant organic traffic in the wellness space
2. **MyGratiBox subscription revival** — if the subscription model is dormant, reactivating it with fresh seasonal boxes could create recurring revenue
3. **Wellness Programs expansion** — define and market these clearly (online courses, group coaching, corporate wellness workshops)
4. **Content upgrades + email funnel** — move beyond 15% discount → build a real nurture sequence
5. **Community building** — Facebook group, membership site, or cohort-based programs
6. **Corporate wellness market** — MyGratiBox as a corporate gift / team wellness tool

### Risks / Concerns
1. **Dormancy signals** — irregular blog publishing and potentially outdated product listings could suggest the business is on pause or low priority
2. **Platform limitations** — Shopify is great for e-commerce but limiting for content/community/courses
3. **Single-voice dependency** — brand is strongly tied to Bev's personal voice; scaling requires either more of her time or expanding the voice
