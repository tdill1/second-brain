# Niche Target List & Prospecting Angles

## Tier 1 — Highest Urgency + Ability to Pay (Start Here)

### 1. HVAC Companies
- **Why they buy:** Every missed call = lost $300-$2,000 emergency job. They're on rooftops and can't answer phones.
- **Pain angle:** "When you're on a roof in July and a call comes in, what happens?"
- **Review angle:** They live and die by Google reviews. Homeowners search "AC repair near me" and pick the top-reviewed company.
- **Avg job value:** $300-$5,000
- **Search terms:** "HVAC near [city]", "AC repair [city]", "heating and cooling [city]"
- **Best time to call:** Early morning (before first job) or after 5pm (proving the missed call problem)

### 2. Plumbers
- **Why they buy:** Emergency-driven. Customer with a burst pipe calls 3 plumbers — the one who responds first wins.
- **Pain angle:** "Speed to respond is everything in plumbing. If you're under a house and a $1,500 job calls in, what happens?"
- **Review angle:** Trust is everything. Homeowners need to feel safe letting someone into their home.
- **Avg job value:** $200-$3,000
- **Search terms:** "plumber near [city]", "emergency plumber [city]"

### 3. Dentists / Dental Offices
- **Why they buy:** Appointment-based business. No-shows cost $200-$500 per empty chair. They need reviews to stand out.
- **Pain angle:** "How many no-shows do you get per week? What does that cost you?"
- **Review angle:** Patients choose dentists based on Google reviews more than almost any other factor.
- **Avg patient lifetime value:** $3,000-$10,000
- **Search terms:** "dentist near [city]", "family dentist [city]"

### 4. Med Spas / Aesthetic Clinics
- **Why they buy:** High-ticket services ($500-$5,000 per treatment). They can't afford slow follow-up. They need social proof.
- **Pain angle:** "A Botox inquiry comes in at 8pm on a Saturday. What happens?"
- **Review angle:** Before/after results + reviews drive 80% of new clients.
- **Avg treatment value:** $500-$5,000
- **Search terms:** "med spa near [city]", "botox [city]", "aesthetic clinic [city]"

## Tier 2 — Strong Fit, Slightly Lower Ticket

### 5. Auto Repair / Body Shops
- **Why they buy:** Customers need fast answers when their car breaks down. Hate admin work, love anything automated.
- **Pain angle:** "When someone's car breaks down at 6pm, they're Googling repair shops and calling down the list. First one to answer wins."
- **Avg job value:** $300-$2,000
- **Search terms:** "auto repair near [city]", "mechanic [city]", "body shop [city]"

### 6. Electricians
- **Why they buy:** Similar to plumbers/HVAC. Emergency jobs, on-site when calls come in.
- **Pain angle:** Same trades pitch — "You're on a job and can't answer. What happens to that call?"
- **Avg job value:** $200-$3,000
- **Search terms:** "electrician near [city]", "electrical contractor [city]"

### 7. Real Estate Agents
- **Why they buy:** Lead response time directly correlates to deals closed. Studies show responding within 5 minutes = 100x better conversion.
- **Pain angle:** "A Zillow lead comes in at 9pm. Do you respond instantly or wait until morning? Because your competitor doesn't wait."
- **Avg commission:** $5,000-$15,000
- **Search terms:** "real estate agent [city]", "realtor near [city]"

### 8. Home Cleaning / Maid Services
- **Why they buy:** Growing businesses overwhelmed with inquiries. Need systems to scale. Review-dependent.
- **Pain angle:** "You're cleaning a house and 3 new inquiries come in. How many of those turn into booked jobs?"
- **Avg job value:** $100-$300 (but recurring)
- **Search terms:** "house cleaning [city]", "maid service near [city]"

## Tier 3 — Good Opportunities, Niche-Specific Angles

### 9. Personal Trainers / Gyms
- **Pain angle:** Membership churn, no-shows, need online booking
- **Review angle:** Testimonials are everything for fitness businesses

### 10. Landscaping Companies
- **Pain angle:** Seasonal rush = missed calls. Need to book fast during peak season.
- **Review angle:** Before/after photos drive referrals

### 11. Roofing Companies
- **Pain angle:** High-ticket ($5K-$20K jobs). One missed call could be their biggest job of the month.
- **Review angle:** Trust factor is massive for letting someone work on your roof.

### 12. Restaurants (Catering Side)
- **Pain angle:** Catering inquiries come in via phone, email, FB, Google — and get lost across channels. The unified inbox is a game-changer.

## Local Prospecting — San Diego / Poway / Eastlake Area

Tony, you have a geographic advantage. Start with businesses in your area:

### Google Maps Search Queries:
- "HVAC Poway" / "HVAC Chula Vista" / "HVAC Eastlake"
- "plumber Poway" / "plumber San Diego"
- "dentist Poway" / "dentist Eastlake"
- "med spa San Diego" / "med spa Chula Vista"
- "auto repair Poway" / "mechanic San Diego"
- "electrician Poway" / "electrician San Diego"

### Look For:
- Under 50 Google reviews (they need review automation)
- No website chat widget
- No "Message" button on Google listing
- Old/outdated website
- Generic voicemail greeting
- Slow or no response when you call after hours

### Red Flags (Skip These):
- Large franchise/chain locations (can't make buying decisions)
- Businesses with 500+ reviews and polished websites (already have systems)
- Businesses not answering during business hours (may be closed/failing)

## Warm Network Prospecting

Before going cold, consider:
- **Booster Club parents** — many are local business owners
- **Your personal network** — who owns or manages a local business?
- **Beverly's network** (TrueSentiments connections)
- **Church/community groups** — business owners in your community
- **Facebook groups** — Poway Business Exchange, Eastlake Community, San Diego Small Business

The fastest path to your first client is almost always someone you already know, or someone one degree removed.
