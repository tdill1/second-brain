# DEI Thesis

## Status
Active — Research and drafting phase

## Description
An academic research thesis examining Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) in a specific organizational or policy context. The project involves literature review, data collection or analysis, and the development of an evidence-based argument contributing to the scholarly conversation around equitable practices. The thesis is expected to meet graduate-level academic standards and culminate in a defended or submitted manuscript.

## Current Priorities
1. **Finalize the thesis statement and research questions** — Narrow the scope to a defensible, specific argument that can be supported within the available timeline and data
2. **Complete the literature review** — Survey the existing body of work on DEI frameworks, outcomes research, and critiques to position the thesis within current scholarship
3. **Establish methodology and data plan** — Decide on qualitative, quantitative, or mixed methods; identify data sources; and outline the analytical approach

## Active Workstreams

### A: Literature Review & Theoretical Framework
- Collect and annotate 30–50 key sources (peer-reviewed articles, policy reports, seminal texts)
- Identify the dominant frameworks in DEI scholarship (e.g., critical race theory, intersectionality, organizational behavior models)
- Write a synthesis of the current state of the field, highlighting gaps the thesis will address

### B: Research Design & Data Collection
- Define the unit of analysis (organization, policy, cohort, etc.)
- Draft methodology section: research design, sampling, instruments, ethical considerations
- If primary research: prepare IRB application and interview/survey instruments
- If secondary analysis: identify and secure access to relevant datasets

### C: Writing & Revision
- Maintain a running outline of all chapters (Introduction, Lit Review, Methods, Findings, Discussion, Conclusion)
- Write in iterative drafts — aim for a complete rough draft before perfecting any single chapter
- Schedule regular check-ins with advisor or committee for feedback

## Next 3 Actions
1. **Write a one-page thesis prospectus** — Thesis statement, 2–3 research questions, proposed method, and expected contribution. Save to `dei-thesis/prospectus.md`
2. **Build an annotated bibliography** — Start with the 10 most critical sources; capture citation, summary, and relevance to the argument. Save to `dei-thesis/annotated-bibliography.md`
3. **Draft the literature review outline** — Organize into thematic sections (not just source-by-source), identifying where the thesis fits in the existing conversation

## Definition of Done
- Thesis manuscript complete (all chapters drafted, revised, and properly cited)
- Methodology executed: data collected, analyzed, and findings written up
- Advisor/committee approval received (or submission to target journal/program completed)
- Final formatted document delivered per institutional or publication requirements
