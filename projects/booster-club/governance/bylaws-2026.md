**EASTLAKE BOYS BASKETBALL BOOSTER CLUB**

**BYLAWS 2026**

# **ARTICLE I – NAME**

The name of the organization shall be **Eastlake Boys Basketball Booster Club**.

# **ARTICLE II – PURPOSE/MISSION STATEMENT**

EHS Boys Basketball Booster Club shall not be organized for profit, with all proceeds directly benefiting the Eastlake High School (EHS) Boys Basketball Program.

The Mission of the Booster Club is to support the EHS Boys Basketball Program by involving the community and families of all basketball players in our various activities, with the common goal of funding and/or providing special services and support to enhance the overall experience for the players, coaches, and fans.

The primary purposes are:

1. To support, promote, and enhance the Boys Basketball Program at Eastlake High School;  
2. To provide financial assistance for travel, tournaments, and other needs;  
3. To foster school spirit, community involvement, sportsmanship, and positive support for players, coaches, and the program;   
4. To organize fundraising activities, events, and volunteer support for the benefit of the boys' basketball program.

The Booster Club shall not interfere with coaching decisions, player selection, playing time, or any coaching/school administrative matters. The Booster Club shall operate in compliance with all school policies, CIF rules, and applicable state/federal laws.

# **ARTICLE III \- GENERAL MEMBERSHIP**

The members of the organization shall be all parents, legal guardians, or interested parties, such as former players, alumni, and community members who wish to support the EHS Boys Basketball Program. Dues-paying members may hold office and participate in elections. Members do not have the authority to make decisions on behalf of the Booster Club, including but not limited to financial decisions and fundraising events, without the Executive Board's consent. 

# **ARTICLE IV – MEETING OF MEMBERS**

Section 1\. Executive Board Meetings will be held at least once per month throughout the school year at such time, place, and manner as determined by the Executive Board. Notice of the meetings shall be publicly disseminated at least 7 days in advance (24 hours for special/urgent meetings).

Section 2\. General meetings will be held quarterly.

# **ARTICLE V \- OFFICERS**

**Number and Title:** The Executive Board of Eastlake Boys Basketball Booster Club must be made up of, but not limited to, the following officers: President, Vice- President, Secretary, and Treasurer.

**Election, Term, Vacancies:** The officers shall be adults nominated by the members of the organization elected to serve on the board from July 1st through June 30th (Except for the inaugural year). Each position is limited to a two-year consecutive term maximum. To avoid conflicts of interest, only one member from each family can serve on the Executive Committee. Officers who run can be alumni parents from prior years as well. Members interested in serving on the Board may be considered after providing a brief candidate’s statement no later than 3 days before the election. The candidate who wins the majority vote is the winner; however, if no candidate wins a majority, the top two candidates receiving the most votes will have a run-off election. For any unfilled seats, nominations will be opened at the time of the election.

**Resignations and/or Removal of Members:** Recognizing that regular attendance is imperative to the success of the Program, Executive Board Officers not attending three (3) consecutive meetings without prior approval of the President may be dismissed. An officer of the organization may be removed with cause by a vote of two-thirds (2/3) of the total Booster Club Members.

**Article VI: POSITIONS/ DUTIES**

a) **The President** (Executive Board Member) shall be the principal officer of the Booster Club. The President shall preside at all meetings of the organization, shall be responsible for seeing that the lines of direction given by the membership are carried into effect, and shall report to the membership on the management of affairs of the organization. The President shall be an ex officio member of all committees established by the membership. The President is responsible for general oversight to ensure the board runs smoothly. The President should meet or be present at all external business-related meetings. All purchases from the booster fund will be requested by the executive members and approved prior to purchase. In the event of a tie during a formal vote on a matter, the President shall cast the deciding vote.

b) **The Vice President** (Executive Board Member) shall serve as the first backup officer to the President and perform the duties of the President at such times as the President is unable to serve at any Booster Club function; perform other duties as requested by the Officers of this Booster Club. In the temporary absence or disability of the President, the Vice-President shall preside at meetings of the organization.

c) **The Secretary** (Executive Board Member) shall be responsible for ensuring that notices of all the meetings of the organization are issued and shall see that minutes of such meetings are kept. The meeting agenda and minutes should be completed and sent out within five business days. The Secretary shall keep meeting records and files and shall exercise such other powers and perform such other Duties as may be assigned by the President. The Secretary creates and sends out marketing communication and other correspondence via database or email as necessary. The Secretary is the primary custodian of the key to the Booster Club mailbox.

d) **The Treasurer** (Executive Board Member) shall be responsible for: 

1. The receipt and custody of all monies of the organization and for the disbursement thereof as authorized;    
2. keeping accurate accounts of monies received and paid out; and   
3. The preparation and issuance of financial statements and reports for monthly reporting to the Booster Club, and approval by vote of the Executive Board.        
4. A summary report of the financial operation of the Booster Club shall be made at least annually by the Treasurer to the general membership and shall be available to the public. The treasurer should deposit all collected funds within two business days. All purchases with booster funds must be requested and approved by the executive board. The Treasurer shall exercise such other duties as may be assigned by the President. 

e) The Communications Manager (Non-Executive Board Member) In consultation with the Board, the Communications Officer’s responsibilities include: developing and implementing internal and external communications. Managing the organization's social media accounts and event coordination, organizing events, and meetings. Monitoring media alerts and Blast communication within the basketball program. A member may volunteer or may be appointed by a board member before a majority vote approval by the Executive Board.

**f) Merchandise Manager** (Non-Executive Board Member) shall manage, overseeing the planning, procurement, production, and sale of merchandise to support the basketball program fundraising efforts and promote school spirit. In consultation with the Board, the Communications Officer’s responsibilities include: selecting a diverse range of merchandise items such as apparel and accessories that appeal to the program members, parents, students, and fans. Sourcing reliable suppliers, manufacturers, and vendors to produce or supply merchandise items. Negotiate pricing, terms, and delivery schedules to ensure cost-effective procurement and timely availability of merchandise inventory. Coordinate the sale and distribution of merchandise. A member may volunteer or may be appointed by a board member before a majority vote approval by the Executive Board.

g) **Team Representatives** (Varsity/ Junior Varsity/ Novice)

i. The Team Representatives shall provide direct oversight of team activities and act as the team liaison on the Booster Club. Representatives are responsible for maintaining an active roster of volunteers to assist in Booster Club events.

Team Representatives will work in support of the mission statement and coordination of Team dinners and other special events. Expenses and fundraising events relating to individual teams require prior consent and authorization from the Executive Board. A member may volunteer or may be appointed by a board member before a majority vote approval by the Executive Board.

ii. **Varsity Representative (Non-Voting Position)** The Varsity Team Representative (“Representative”) will assist in the coordination of any special events taking place at the varsity games, including Senior Night. The Varsity Representative will create a contact list of parents' and players' contact information and will be responsible for notifying parents/players of all relevant team activity, including, but not limited to: team dinners, team lunches, special events, fundraisers, etc.

iii. **Novice and Junior Varsity Representative: (Non-Voting Position)** Novice and Junior Varsity Team Representative (“Representative”) will assist with the coordination of any special events taking place at the Novice/Junior Varsity home games and team dinners.  The Representative will create a contact list of parent and player contact information and will be responsible for notifying parents/players of all relevant team activity, including, but not limited to: team dinners, special events, fundraisers, etc.

h) **The Concession Coordinator** (non-executive/ non-voting member) shall manage the Snack Bar Operation during the season, working with the President and Treasurer in the management of inventory and staffing. The Treasurer will oversee cash management. The Concession Coordinator shall coordinate with the Treasurer to obtain the cash box(s) at each home game. To count and verify each box with an Executive Board Member and then return the funds to the Treasurer. The Concession Coordinator will ensure the Snack Bar has coverage for all home games. The Concession Coordinator shall get prior approval from the Executive Board for any operational changes to the flow of the snack bar. A member may volunteer or may be appointed by a board member before a majority vote approval by the Executive Board.

i) **Head Coach:** (Non-voting member) Shall serve as the main liaison of this Booster Club to the faculty and staff of Eastlake High School; Represent the Booster Club, along with the President, at meetings of any other groups where the purposes and actions of this Booster Club have pertinence; Serve the Booster Club as the principal authority and advisor as to the needs and priorities in the programs and facilities at Eastlake High School. This position is advisory only with no voting authority.

**ARTICLE VII \- COMMITTEES**

**Establishment:** The Board may establish standing or special committees as it deems necessary. Such committees shall have names, powers, duties, and existence as determined by the Executive Board.

**Appointment and Term of Chairperson:** The Chairperson of committees shall be appointed by the President, subject to approval of the Executive Board, for a term of one (1) school year. The Chairperson of each committee shall be appointed from among the membership.

Appointment and Term of Members: The committee members shall be appointed by the President after consultation with the Executive Board and Committee Chairperson and subject to the approval of the organization for a term of one (1) school year. 

**Voting:** All voting shall be determined by a majority rule, unless otherwise stipulated.

**ARTICLE VIII \- EXECUTIVE BOARD**

Composition: The Executive Board shall include the President, Vice- President, Secretary, and Treasurer.

**Responsibilities:** The Executive Board may exercise the powers of the Booster Club in between meetings. The Executive Board shall develop a budget of operations for the school year (July 1 – June 30, each school year) for adoption by the Booster Club. The Executive Board shall not have the power to take action that is contrary to, or a substantial departure from, the direction established by the Booster Club, or which represents a major change in the affairs, business, or current policy. The Executive Board shall submit reports on actions taken to the general Booster Club Membership.

**Meetings:** Special meetings of the Executive Board shall be called at the discretion of the Executive Board. 

**ARTICLE IX \- MISCELLANEOUS**

**Approved Signatures:** All contracts, checks, and money orders for the payment, receipt or deposit of money and access to the securities of the Booster Club shall be handled as provided by resolution of The President. The President, Vice President, Secretary and Treasurer are authorized signers on the Booster Club checking account.

**Expenditures:** The Executive Board must approve any expenditures from the Booster Club funds in advance. Prior to any expenditure, at least three quotes must be submitted to the Executive Board for approval of the purchase.

**Board Turnover:** The current board will provide a smooth transition to the incoming board and provide all historical and current documentation. Each Executive Board Member will meet with the incoming board member to ensure they are prepared prior to the beginning of the school year.

**Dissolution:** In the event of dissolution of the organization, assets of the organization remaining after the discharge of all liabilities shall be placed in trust to benefit the Eastlake Boys Basketball Program.

**ARTICLE X \- AMENDMENTS**

These bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds majority vote of those present and voting at any meeting of the Board at which a quorum is present, provided that the proposal shall have been included in the notice of the meeting. Booster Club Bylaws are amended and approved by the Eastlake Boys Basketball Booster Club members and officers.
